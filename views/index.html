The Math Anxiety Research Project

Many people experience math anxiety, especially related to testing situations. Even adult learners experience math anxiety. But measuring math anxiety can help researchers, administrators and psychologists better understand it and develop appropriate interventions and treatments.

Relationships Affect Learning

Relationships can enhance learning, but they can also suppress it. This is the case when parents and teachers unintentionally pass on negative views about math. No parent or teacher wants to impart feelings of anxiety and aversion, but that’s often what happens.

Can it be prevented?

Many people experience nervousness or anxiety when they are required to perform mathematical tasks, just as many people have fears of snakes or tall buildings. And like those other fears, being aware of them is the first step to changing them. And that’s what the Math Anxiety Reference Scale survey can do.

Cool Sub-title

Being anxious can make students avoid doing anything requiring math. This limits their development of skills and prevents them from taking on many professional opportunities later in life.

But you can help. Take this survey so researchers, administrators and psychologists can learn more about adult math anxiety so they can turn the tide and save our children.
